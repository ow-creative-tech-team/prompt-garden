# Brand Voice Reviewers

This folder contains reviewer prompts for checking content against brand voice, house style, naming, structure, and channel expectations.

Use these prompts when you want editorial feedback before publishing, sending, presenting, or sharing content. Choose the reviewer by content type.

| Reviewer | Use when | Do not use when |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `tone-of-voice-reviewer-prompt.md` | Reviewing short copy, labels, captions, standalone paragraphs, or quick tone checks. | Reviewing a full email, full publication, full presentation, or social post. |
| `email-reviewer-prompt.md` | Reviewing internal, external, client, or executive email drafts. | The content is not structured as an email. |
| `publication-reviewer-prompt.md` | Reviewing long-form or structured editorial content, including articles, blog posts, website copy, newsletters, reports, or internal communications. | The content is mainly a slide deck, email, or social post. |
| `presentation-reviewer-prompt.md` | Reviewing full presentations, slide-by-slide copy, narrative flow, executive readiness, and visual-brand alignment. | You only need a quick sentence-level tone check. |
| `social-media-reviewer-prompt.md` | Reviewing LinkedIn, X, or other professional social media drafts before posting. | The content is long-form editorial, email, or a full presentation. |

## How To Use

1. Pick the reviewer that matches the content format.
2. Paste the content to review.
3. Include any context that affects the review, such as audience, channel, purpose, publication date, or required call to action.
4. Ask for either a full review or a focused check, such as tone only, house style only, or naming only.

## Standalone Guidance

The reviewer prompts are designed to work as standalone system prompts. Each prompt should include the brand voice, house style, naming, channel, and review rules needed for its use case.

Do not assume separate brand reference files will be uploaded with the prompt. If official guidance changes, update the relevant reviewer prompt so it remains self-contained.

Visual-brand review belongs primarily in `presentation-reviewer-prompt.md`, which should include any visual rules required for presentation review.
