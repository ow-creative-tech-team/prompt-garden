# Brand Voice Reference

Use this only when reviewing voice, copy, house style, or writing mechanics.

## Voice

- **Clear**: Lead with the point. Use plain language.
- **Credible**: Ground claims in evidence. Be precise.
- **Confident**: Use active voice. State conclusions directly.
- **Objective, not cold**: Analytical but human. Balanced, not sales-driven.
- **Concise**: Use tight sentences and remove filler.

## Personality

- **Confident**: Bold points of view, especially in headlines; firm statements.
- **Approachable**: Explain jargon. Be welcoming.
- **Forward-leaning**: Frame challenges as solvable risks or growth opportunities.

## Presentation Copy

- Use short, declarative sentences.
- Prefer action-oriented headings that state the "so what."
- Use sentence case for headlines, headings, and graphic labels.
- Keep bullet lists short, parallel, and easy to scan.

## House Style

- AP Style is the writing baseline.
- Use US English by default unless content is local-only.
- Use the Oxford comma.
- Spell out one through nine; use numerals for 10+.
- Use `%` with numerals.
- Define acronyms on first mention if reused.
- Do not use periods in acronyms such as US, UK, EU, CEO.
- Do not use `&` instead of "and" except in official names and accepted acronyms such as D&O and M&A.
- Use Month Day, Year. Do not use ordinals.
- Use `2 pm` style times; use noon and midnight.
- Prefer inline citations; use endnotes when notes are necessary.

## Naming

- Use **Oliver Wyman** in full. Do not use **OW**.
- After first mention, **the company** is acceptable and lowercase.
- Refer to people internally as **colleagues**, not employees.
- Use `it/its` for a single entity unless referring to its members.

## Avoid

- Corporate-speak, inflated claims, unsupported hype, and unnecessary qualifiers.
- Overuse of acronyms and abbreviations.
- Sales-driven language that weakens objectivity.
