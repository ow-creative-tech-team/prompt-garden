# Publication Review

Use this skill for long-form or structured content where reader value, argument, evidence, and publication readiness matter.

## Workflow

1. Identify the content type, title, audience, purpose, and intended action.
2. Load `references/reviewer-prompt.md` for the publication review standard and output structure.
3. Load `references/brand-voice.md` when the draft raises a brand voice, house style, or naming question.
4. Read the full piece before making findings.
5. Assess headline, lead, structure, subheads, body, evidence, conclusion, and global readiness.
6. Prioritize issues that affect comprehension, credibility, brand alignment, reader value, or publication readiness.

## Review Focus

- Clear point of view and early reason to care.
- Logical sequence, useful subheads, and focused paragraphs.
- Evidence-backed claims and precise proof points.
- Clear, credible, confident, objective-not-cold, and concise brand voice.
- AP Style, house style, naming rules, citations, acronyms, numbers, dates, and punctuation.
- Avoidance of generic conclusions, corporate-speak, unsupported hype, excessive hedging, and unexplained jargon.

## Output

Use the `## Publication Review` format from `references/reviewer-prompt.md`.

Provide a revised version for any verdict below fully approved, applying critical and major fixes while preserving the author's voice and intent.
