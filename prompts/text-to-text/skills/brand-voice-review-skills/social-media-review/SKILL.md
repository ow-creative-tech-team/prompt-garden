# Social Media Review

Use this skill for LinkedIn posts, X posts, executive social copy, campaign posts, event posts, research promotion, colleague recognition, and other social media drafts.

## Workflow

1. Identify the platform, audience, content type, and intended action.
2. Load `references/reviewer-prompt.md` for the social media review standard and output structure.
3. Load `references/brand-voice.md` when the draft raises a brand voice, house style, or naming question.
4. Check whether the post earns attention from senior business audiences with a clear reason to care.
5. Prioritize issues that affect publishability, credibility, platform fit, audience value, brand alignment, or global readiness.
6. Preserve the author's intent and point of view.

## Review Focus

- Strong opening sentence before feed truncation.
- Clear "so what" and audience value.
- Platform-appropriate length, line breaks, hashtags, mentions, and calls to action.
- Clear, credible, confident, objective-not-cold, and concise brand voice.
- AP Style, house style, naming rules, and sentence case.
- Avoidance of unsupported hype, corporate-speak, noisy hashtags, idioms, slang, and unexplained acronyms.

## Output

Use the `## Social Media Review` format from `references/reviewer-prompt.md`.

Provide a complete revised post unless the content is publication-ready without changes.
